COVID-19 Vaccine Information

As of October 1, 2021, there have been more than 10 manufacturers producing COVID-19 vaccines, and the total number of people vaccinated globally has exceeded 3.5 billion, which is a considerable number. But the vaccination rate varies greatly from country to country. Why is that? Let's explore together.

How many percentages of people are vaccinated in different countries?

According to this choropleth map, in most countries around the world, the percentage of the population vaccinated in Canada, China, and Brazil exceeds 70%. At the same time, as of October 1,2021, Australia's vaccinated rate has reached 65%.

Which regions have the most of their percentage population vaccinated?

It can be seen form this plot that in East Asia and the Western European region, the vaccination rate is generally high, and the median of the two regions is over 70%. However, Sub-Saharan Africa has the lowest vaccination rate, with the highest value being less than 10%. The huge differences in the percentage of the population vaccinated in different regions may be affected by the local economy and culture.

Does a higher GDP mean a higher vaccinated population?

According to this scatter plot, it can be concluded that there is a positive correlation between GDP per capita and percentage of population vaccinated.
